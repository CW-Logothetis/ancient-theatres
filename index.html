EXPLORE THE

ANCIENT
THEATRES

of ALBANIA and GREECE

 

Although seated areas existed in Minoan times (2500-1600 BC), the tradition of ancient Greek theatre began around 700BC.

Yet it wasn't until 450BC-350BC that the enormous stone theatres that remain today were constructed for performances.

They would soon hold large gatherings - as well as drama - and no doubt "witnessed the birth of democracy".

Later, during the Hellenistic period (after Alexander the Great) the theatres were adapted to host Games, together with new stadia.

And as time passed and the Romans conquered the region, existing buildings were expanded and many new ones built.

But here, we look at some of our favourite ancient Greek theatres
